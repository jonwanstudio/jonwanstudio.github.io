Helmholtz Resonator

Acoustic Engineering & Prototyping

About This Project

This project is an acoustic engineering endeavor focused on the design and prototyping of a custom Helmholtz resonator. It is specifically engineered to target and eliminate a specific, low-frequency acoustic resonance—commonly known as "exhaust drone"—that occurs within a set RPM range.

The core challenge is that the target frequency is not static; it is dependent on the exhaust gas temperature (EGT). Our methodology addresses this by first capturing empirical data (EGT) using a thermal gun at the exact RPM the drone occurs. This temperature data is used to calculate the precise speed of sound (c) within the exhaust, allowing us to pinpoint the exact target frequency (f).

The resonator's dimensions are then calculated using the Helmholtz resonance frequency equation:

f = (c / 2π) × √(S / ( l · V0))
  • f = Target Frequency (Hz)
  • c = Speed of Sound (m/s)
  • S = Cross-sectional Area of the Neck (m²)
  • l = Length of the Neck (m)
  • V0 = Volume of the Chamber (m³)

For real-world accuracy, the simple neck length (l) is insufficient. This design refined the calculation by using an effective neck length (leff), which accounts for the "end correction" (the mass of air vibrating just outside the neck opening). This corrected formula provides a much more accurate model for tuning.

This methodology resulted in the final prototype design, engineered to cancel the target drone frequency:

  • Chamber: 10" length x 3.5" outer diameter
  • Neck: 5" length x 1" outer diameter

This is a 3D printed prototype mockup for fitment purposes, as the actually product will be made out of metal.

Key Features

  • Acoustic Analysis: Targets and cancels specific, low-frequency exhaust drone.
  • Data-Driven Design: Uses empirical exhaust gas temperature (EGT) to calculate the speed of sound.
  • Physics-Based Modeling: Governed by the Helmholtz resonance equation, including "End Correction" for accuracy.
  • Functional Prototype: Resulted in a specific, dimensioned design (10"x3.5" chamber, 5"x1" neck) for real-world fabrication.
  • Application: Automotive acoustics and exhaust system refinement.
